    16 Apr 2024, 00:00

    THE family of late comedian Victoria Wood are caught up in a feud over a memorial tribute.

    Her brother is rowing with other relatives about the design of the sculpture to the star, who died of cancer last April aged 62.

    Chris Foote Wood, 76, has raised £23,000 of the £30,000 needed to fund a life-size statue he wants of Victoria as her character Bren from BBC sitcom Dinnerladies.

    Dame Judi Dench, 82, has pledged £1,000 for the work, to be sited in Victoria’s home town of Bury.

    The council has provided the land and agreed to maintain the statue once it is erected.

    But Victoria’s children Grace, 28, and Henry Durham, 24, say they want a “tasteful” work and claim they have not been properly ­consulted on the design or choice of artist.

    A source accused Mr Foote Wood of “firing off under his own steam”.

    Both sides met on Friday and her children were given more control, it was reported.

    Author Mr Foote Wood said before the talks: “When the statue is finished it will belong to mebecause I’ve paid for it.

    “I’m putting up the money with the help of crowd-funding. When it’s up and running I’ll gift the statue to the council because it should belong to the people of Bury.”

    Mr Foote Wood, who faced a backlash last year after calling his late sister a fat, unhappy girl in a biography, said: “Things are proceeding, but I can’t say it’s exactly straightforward.”

    A Wood family source said after the talks: “The estate was represented and will now take instructions from the family.

    “This isn’t the first time he (Mr Foote Wood) has gone firing off under his own steam.” The family would not make any further comment.
