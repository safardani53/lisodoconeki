    THG readers know that we love Amy Roloff. We adore her social media, her books, her food, and her smile that lights up a room.

    We’ve also followed Amy’s romance with Chris Marek closely, which makes her recent NSFW caption so much more entertaining.

    In an otherwise innocuous post about pizza, Amy shared a little TMI. Take a look:

    "Afternoon delight," Amy’s captions for this black-and-white photo begin, followed by music note emojis.

    She writes that she is "taking a rare moment in the middle of the day just to hang out."

    Amy took the snap "at one of my favorite places downtown Hillsboro @pizzario, w/ Chris."

    They were there to eat "and talk, appreciate each other and the day. It was wonderful!"

    Amy is "all about making moments [together]."

    As you can imagine, a number of fans raised their eyebrows at "afternoon delight."

    If you’re not aware, the term is a widely used euphamism for sex in the middle of the day.

    When one fan asked, Amy replied: "now now … that didn’t happen."

    "It’s in a song," Amy acknowledged. "But can’t remember all the words."

    Literally no part of the lyrics is at all subtle about the subject matter. The song’s about boning.

    "Anyway," Amy continued in he reply. "That didn’t happen.’"

    Just a couple of weeks ago, Amy shared a brunch photo of her with Chris — with a much less suggestive caption.

    "Sunday brunch with Chris," Amy announced on Instagram.

    She admitted: "I was bummed my offer on a house got rejected."

    "I was thankful Chris was with me," Amy expressed. "And the comfort he gave to just quietly help me to let it go."

    "And," she continued. "Tell me there’s going to be another house I can make a home."

    Amy may have been feeling down, but she knows her coping strategies.

    "One way for me to get out of a rut is to cook," Amy shared.

    "So," her caption continued. "We had a nice quiet wonderful Sunday having brunch,"

    Amy truly enjoyed Chris and "a cup of coffee, and just being together."

    "Thanks babe," she concluded. "Life is still good."

    Amy has gotten a lot of grief — even from her own fans — for her relationship with Chris Marek.

    Some fans were "convinced" that he was just out to scam Amy out of her reality TV money.

    Others insisted that he "must" be after fame.

    Some of these fans were just attached to Amy and Matt as a couple and were having trouble letting go.

    But others clearly had some disturbing ideas about little people — and somehow believed that a man of average height couldn’t genuinely love Amy.

    Fortunately, those people were way, way off.

    These days, when fans bother Amy about her romance, it takes a very different tone.

    Namely, fans want to see her get hitched to Chris and they want to know when he’s going to pop the question.

    Amy has suggested that she is open to getting married, but doesn’t want to rush into it.

    She takes marriage very seriously and wants to get married for life — when the time is right.

    In the mean time, she’ll just stick to "afternoon delight."
