    Adele Reveals She Quit Drinking 3 Months Ago

    Adele is opening up about her sober journey. The Grammy-winning singer, 35, told fans at her residency show in Las Vegas over the weekend that she cut alcohol out of her life more than three months ago.

    01 Jan 0001, 00:00

    Adele is opening up about her sober journey.

    The Grammy-winning singer, 35, told fans at her residency show in Las Vegas over the weekend that she cut alcohol out of her life more than three months ago.

    “I stopped drinking maybe like three and a half months ago,” Adele said at her Weekends with Adele show, according to a report by the Daily Mail. 

    While the singer said “it's boring” to quit drinking, she admitted to the audience that she consumed too much alcohol in her younger years.

    “I mean, I was literally borderline alcoholic for quite a lot of my 20s,” Adele revealed. “But I miss it so much.”

    The “I Drink Wine” singer also shared during her concert that she “cut out caffeine.” 

    “So enjoy your whiskey sour. I'm very, very jealous,” Adele told a fan at the show who said that they had been drinking all day before attending.

    The singer previously revealed that she “stopped drinking” following her divorce from ex-husband Simon Konecki and death of her father Mark Evans from bowel cancer in 2021, during her CBS special interview with Oprah Winfrey.

    "That’s one great way of really sort of getting to know yourself, is just drinking water and being sober as anything,” she told Winfrey, 69, in the special.

    During her residency concert over the weekend, Adele spilled the details about her birthday plans for son Angelo — who turns 11 on Thursday.

    “We have got a busy week coming celebrating him. He is very much like me because I take my birthday very seriously,” Adele said, per Mail Online. The “Easy on Me” Singer — who is currently datin sports agent Rich Paul — shares son Angelo with ex Konecki. 

    Adele added that her son will have “a whole month celebration” to mark his upcoming birthday.  

    “From a very young age he seems to have adopted the same point of view, which isn’t quite as fun,” she continued. “He is my son and, obviously, I adore him and he is like, ‘Can I do this and do that?’ "

    '“I am like, ‘That is very expensive. That is my week, so I guess my update next week will be, being a bullied mother probably!”
