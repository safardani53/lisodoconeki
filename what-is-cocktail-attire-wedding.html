    COCKTAIL is one of the most common attires to be requested of wedding guests.

    We're here to help ensure you nail the look and avoid any fashion faux pas, no matter the ceremony's time, season, or venue.

    What is cocktail attire for a wedding?

    The idea of cocktail attire is to look classy without getting too formal.

    It's more elevated than anything you'd wear to the office - but nothing nearly as formal as what you'd see on Bridgerton.

    You want to look elegant without upstaging the bride, groom, and remaining wedding party.

    The dress code need not hamper your creativity or personal style; accessories, patterns, and shoes are easy ways to add flair.

    How does one wear cocktail attire?

    One of the most important things to consider for cocktail attire is the hem length of your dress.

    This isn't an occasion for mini skirts or floor-length ball gowns.

    Opt for somewhere in-between - from right above your knees to around your calves.

    Jumpsuits are also acceptable, as they aren't as formal as gown-style dresses.

    MOST READ IN FASHION

    A suit and tie is the go-to look for men's wedding cocktail attire.

    Save the tuxedos for the groom and groomsmen.

    However, this is an excellent chance to break out a bow tie with a funky print or wear a mismatched belt and shoes.

    From an etiquette point of view: even if it's hot, men who are guests should stay in their suits until the groom removes his.

    Does the season matter regarding wedding cocktail attire?

    The color, material, and accessories of your outfit will likely depend on the season.

    For spring and summer weddings, abstract or floral prints are always a good option.

    You might also consider a solid pastel dress for the spring or a brighter color for the summer.

    Just ensure you're not wearing the same color the bridesmaids will be in.

    For men, it can be dashing to play with fabrics - like seersucker or linen - or even a patterned, colored suit.

    While dressy sandals are acceptable for women, men shouldn't wear sandals to a cocktail occasion.

    For a fall wedding, you can't go wrong with jewel tones for your outfit.

    Add a pop of gold with jewelry, bags, belt, or shoes to finiff the look.

    As for winter weddings, this is your time to shine.

    Winter weddings are one of the only times when sequins, fringe, and shiny fabrics won't stick out as being too fancy or overpowering - so embrace them.
