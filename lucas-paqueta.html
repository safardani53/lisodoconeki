    Lucas Tolentino Coelho de Lima, popular as Lucas Paqueta, is a soccer player from Brazil who plays as a midfielder for Premier League Club West Ham United and the Brazilian national team. 

    The outstanding midfielder was lost in Milan, reborn at Lyon, and later became the most expensive transfer of West Ham United with a fee of about £51 million ($61 million).

    Nicknamed from the Paqueta Island, where he was born, Paqueta has been capped 35 times by Brazil.

    The star athlete has played for famous clubs like Flamengo, AC Milan, and Lyon. He is currently playing for West Ham United.

    Lucas Paqueta | Family & Education

    Tolentino Coelho de Lima was born on 27 August 1997 in Paqueta Island, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.

    His father’s name is Marcelo de Lima, and his mother’s name is Cristiane Tolentino. Cristiane is a very supportive mother. She quit her hair dressing-business to follow wherever his young son’s career took him.

    Paqueta has a brother named Matheus, who is also a soccer player. Matheus plays from Brazilian club Tombense.

    There is no information on his educational qualifications.

    Lucas Paqueta | Early Life

    When Lucas Paqueta was ten years old, he arrived at Flamengo.

    His technical skills were excellent, but he was only 5 feet tall and lacked the physicality like other players of his age. 

    The Flamengo youth team, with which Paqueta was associated, won the 2016 Copa So Paulo de Juniors, Brazil’s most important youth tournament. 

    After the competition, Lucas was promoted to the club’s professional team. 

    Lucas Paqueta | Professional Career

    Flamengo

    Paqueta made his professional debut through Club Flamengo in March 2016. 

    At the Estádio Raulino de Oliveira in Volta Redon on 19 February 2017, Paqueta scored his first professional goal in a 4–0 win over Madureira.  

    Further, Lucas Paqueta was selected to the team of the 2018 Campeonato Carioca as the best left-winger.

    Flamengo fans considered him, along with Juan, to be the club’s highlight in the Copa Sudamericana final.

    AC Milan

    In 2019, AC Milan and Flamengo agreed to a €35 million ($36.26 million) deal to transfer Paquet.

    On 4 January 2019, the star player signed a five-year contract with AC Milan. He wore kit number 39 for the club. 

    Paqueta scored his first goal for Milan in a 3–0 win against Cagliari, scoring the second goal.

    He dedicated his first goal at Milan to the Flamengo under-15 team members, who were engulfed by fire at his old club.

    Lyon

    In September 2020, French club Lyon announced the transfer of Paqueta for €20 million ($20.728 million).

    Paqueta was reborn at Lyon as a star. He was elected the best foreign player in the French Championship, Ligue 1, during the 21/22 season.

    West Ham United

    In August 2022, West Ham United signed Paqueta for a club-record fee.

    BBC estimated the transfer cost to be about £51 million ($61 million).

    He made the new club and Premier League debut on 31 August 2022. The young athlete currently plays for the club.

    Lucas Paqueta | International Debut

    In 2018, coach Tite of the Brazilian national team chose him as one of the twelve alternates for the Brazilian national team for the 2018 FIFA World Cup in Russia.

    He debuted for his country on September 7, 2018, when he came off the bench to take Philippe Coutinho’s place in the 71st minute of a friendly match against the United States.

    Paqueta was the youngest of the 35 players listed on the 23-man squad and provisional roster.

    Will Lucas Paqueta Play FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022?

    Yes. He has been included in a 26-man squad of the Brazil national team (nicknamed Seleção Canarinha) for the 2022 FIFA World Cup.

    The athlete is heading to Qatar to play in the World Cup, which begins on 20th November 2022.

    In the 2022 FIFA World Cup South American qualifiers, Paqueta scored the only goal in the 72nd minute, and the Seleção won the match against Colombia by 1-0.

    Paqueta is a promising player and is a strong support to the Seleção squad.

    His current club West Ham United has wished him good luck for the FIFA World Cup finals.
