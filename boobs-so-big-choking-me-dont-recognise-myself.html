    MANY people would love to have bigger breasts.

    But one woman has undergone a 'life-changing' procedure after she revealed her boobs were so big they were choking her.

    Annie Anderson said her mammary glands had become so restrictive that she was no longer able to go on theme park rides.

    The 49-year-old used to suffer with her 44E cup size breasts as a teenager, where she claims boys used to grope her and make sexual comments.

    Growing up in California, US, she said she focused on hiding her body and hated the way she looked.

    The nurse turned to eating to find comfort and soon started to gain weight.

    She said she struggled to sleep on her back as it felt like they were "choking" her.

    Annie finally decided to have surgery and is celebrating her transformation.

    "It was shocking – I lived with those breasts my entire life and they were massive.

    "It's a total mind f**k to have your body change overnight and, in my eyes, I don't see myself as I am.

    "I had my first consult with a plastic surgeon in 2012 and at that time, very few surgeons were doing plus-size surgeries.

    "They wanted me to lose 50lbs and maintain that loss for over a year before surgery and I lost, but gained [this weight] over and over again," Annie said.

    She added that previously, doctors had told her she had polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).

    This can cause obesity and make it hard to maintain weight loss.

    But she eventually managed to find a surgeon who didn't ask about her weight and said they would do her surgery despite her body mass index (BMI).

    It's unbelievable the pain we cause ourselves, all while pretending to be OK

    Many surgeons will refuse to operate on someone whose BMI is over a certain rate - as this can be dangerous.

    But Annie said her doctor looked at all the risk factors in order to make a decision.

    She said that before the surgery, buying swimsuits and dresses had always been difficult.

    "I could never wear the cute sundresses I wanted to because I could never go without a bra and a strapless one didn't hold me up enough.

    "Practically every shirt I owned had a stain on the chest [as well] because it was like eating with a shelf in the way.

    "Most exercise was painful and embarrassing and I've never been able to jog or run.

    "It's unbelievable the pain we cause ourselves, all while pretending to be OK," Annie said.

    BOOKED IN

    Annie booked the procedure in March 2022 and was able to get it for free as her health insurance covered the surgery.

    But she highlighted that her recovery hasn't been easy, with delays.

    She said: "They say it will be up to a year before I will see and feel the final results – but I've been cleared to wear underwire bras once again and can go without if I'd like.

    "I still have swelling and major bloating by the end of the day, but my back, neck and shoulder pain are gone.

    "I'm already way more consistently active than I was before the surgery and I walk or swim most days."

    She has recently been measured for a new bra and is a 42C - with the doctor claiming he removed 2.5lbs from each breast.

    GOODBYE OLD FRIENDS

    In order to let go of her old boobs, she had a goodbye party.

    Annie said they had gotten her lots of attention over the years.

    Her friends ordered a plaster cast kit and they all casted their own breasts to make her feel more comfortable.

    Sharing the before and after of her transformation, Annie decided to upload a video of her dancing with the cast – which has racked up three million views and 72,000 likes.

    Annie now says she wishes she'd had the procedure sooner as she spent so many years hating her body.

    "[Now], I get so many compliments on my appearance and most people are extremely supportive.
