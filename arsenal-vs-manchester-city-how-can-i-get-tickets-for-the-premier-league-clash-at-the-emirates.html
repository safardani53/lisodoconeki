    Arsenal vs Manchester City: How can I get tickets for the Premier League clash at the Emirates?

    ARSENAL and Manchester City will play twice more this season, once in the final of the Carabao Cup and once at the Emirates. And the latter meet could be just as hard-fought, as Arsenal look to secure a top four place and Manchester City the Premier League crown.

    04 Jun 2024, 00:00

    ARSENAL and Manchester City will play twice more this season, once in the final of the Carabao Cup and once at the Emirates.

    And the latter meet could be just as hard-fought, as Arsenal look to secure a top four place and Manchester City the Premier League crown.

    How can I get tickets for Arsenal vs Manchester City?

    Club Arsenal tickets for the game are still available for the clash at the Emirates through Thomas Cook.

    Although it's worth noting that they don’t come cheap.

    It’s £999 a ticket for premium tickets for this game, which includes entry into one of the VIP executive boxes, the chance to meet an Arsenal legend and a three-course meal with free drinks all day.

    However, if you’re feeling flush this month, you can buy tickets here.

    There’s also tickets available for Arsenal vs West Ham on April 21, also in an executive box, with tickets costing £719 which can be bought from here.

    Or if you’re keen to Man City in action this season, Thomas Cook has general admission tickets along with a hotel stay to see Guardiola’s side play Chelsea, FC Basel in the Champions League and Manchester United.

    All of those tickets can be found here.

    Arsenal vs Manchester City analysis

    By the time this fixture comes around, Man City could have the Premier League all but wrapped up.

    Pep Guardiola’s side currently have a 12-point lead over the chasing pack and haven’t showed much sign of slowing down.

    And with highly-rated Atletico Madrid defender Aymeric Laporte potentially joining the Etihad outfit by then, City’s squad could be unstoppable.

    Arsenal too might have a new-look squad by early March.

    More from Sport

    They’ve already added Man United cast-off Henrikh Mkhitaryan to their ranks in a swap deal for wantaway Alexis Sanchez.

    And Arsene Wenger’s side are also being linked with a move for Borussia Dortmund striker Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang, who’s angling for a move away from Germany.

    The Gunners will be hoping these acquisitions are enough to catapult them past their top six rivals into the Champions League spots.
