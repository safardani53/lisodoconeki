    Men are getting lip filler injected into their penises to add inches - DONT, doctors warn

    IT'S COMMON for dermal filler to be used to plump up lips or add volume to parts of the face. But now doctors have been forced to warn men to think twice before resorting to such methods to add a few inches to their peckers.

    20 Sep 2024, 00:00

    IT'S COMMON for dermal filler to be used to plump up lips or add volume to parts of the face.

    But now doctors have been forced to warn men to think twice before resorting to such methods to add a few inches to their peckers.

    It comes as more men are opting for the 'quick fix'.

    Paul, a 39-year-old living in Texas contemplated penis enlargement surgery when he overheard his ex-wife making fun of his manhood during the breakdown of their marriage.

    But then he stumbled upon came across an anti-aging clinic in the area advertising penile fillers in May 2022.

    The procedures promised to be reversible because they involved injecting a dissolvable gel, in the way lip filler might be administered.

    Paul, who only gave his first name, told DailyMail.com he made down payment of $2,500 for an initial consultation.

    "I knew nothing about the procedure other than what was posted online... but I wanted it so badly."

    He told the website he knew shouldn't have paid the deposit, but that the add gave him a 'sliver of hope'.

    Paul was hoping for a natural girth enhancement that partners wouldn't be able to point out, but instead, he ended up with a "big, squishy, lumpy disaster".

    "It basically looked like I had a pencil on either side [of my penis], where they had just inserted this big, long, straight tube of filler. It was so obvious and unnatural looking," he said.

    "It made me very depressed. I was like, what have I done? I've ruined something that I should not have messed with."

    Paul was able to get the botched procedure corrected at Dallas-based cosmetic clinic PhalloFILL, which completes penis filler procedures using hyaluronic acid.

    During the 30-minute treatment, patients are numbed with shots of local anesthetic, and then dermal filler is injected underneath the penis’s top layer of skin.

    There is typically some bruising and swelling right after the procedure.

    Paul told DailyMail.com that the corrective procedure radically changed his sex life for the better.

    Felllow Texan Jordan Tunnell, 33, also out the procedure after years of feeling insecure about his penis size.

    The structural engineer said he's tried using a penis pump after feeling partners weren't sexually satisfied, with little results.

    After getting penile fillers at PhalloFILL, Jordan said his was now more of a 'shower' - it used a shrink a lot - and his sensitivity has increased.

    He even convinced a few friends to get it done themselves, he added.

    In the US, where Paul and Jordan got their procedures done, filler is cleared for use in lips by the Food and Drug Administration, but providers use it off-label for penile injections.

    While penis fillers can be reversed with a dissolving injection, there is limited research in the risks involved in injecting them in this location.

    Like with facial fillers, solution that is inexpertly injected into or around an artery could cause tissue to die.

    And in the first week after enlargement injections, there is also the risk of filler migration, where the filler moves from the original injection site, resulting in an irregular shape or feel.

    If the filler is not applied correctly, it can also harden and leave permanent disfigurement, or the injection sites may get infected.

    Private clinic International Andrology London said: that while lumping or migration is unusual for filler used to smooth wrinkles is unusual due to the small amounts administered, "this is not necessarily the case for penis enlargement".

    "Firstly, quite a lot of filler is required (10 to 40ml depending on the penis size) and also since the penis changes shape and size during sexual activity, the risk of lumping or migration is considerable."

    It also added that results from the procedure could fade as early as six months afterwards, adding that most of its patients have filler top-ups one a year.

    Andrology charges between £500 for 10ml of filler, to £2500 for 20ml, depending on the brand used.

    Meanwhile, Androfil priced its penis filling between £1750 to £2800, depending on the filler quality.

    Various clinics in the UK offer non-surgical penis filler procedures, using hyaluronic acid or synthetic fillers.

    According to the NHS, the average adult penis size is usually between 3in (8.5cm) and 4in (10.5cm) long when soft, and 6in (15cm) to 7in (18cm) when hard.

    Enlargement surgery is available on the NHS, but only if you have a micro-penis.
