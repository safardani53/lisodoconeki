    A MUM has revealed her 'immeasurable grief' after her eight-year-old son was killed in a lorry smash on a smart motorway after visiting his critically ill brother.

    Meera Naran's son Dev died when a truck ploughed into his grandad's car on the M6 on the way home from Birmingham Children's Hospital.

    They had raced to see Mrs Naran's son Neel, 11, after doctors told the mum he may not live through the night.

    Dev was killed instantly when his grandad's car stopped on the inside lane and was hit 45 seconds later on May 31 2018.

    Mrs Naran, from Leicester, told Metro.co.uk: "We’ve lost our baby. Eight years we raised a child, through the sleepless nights, through the colds and the coughs.

    "To lose that overnight is just heart-breaking.

    "Dev was very academic, he was captain of the football team, the rugby team, the cricket team. He excelled at school and he wanted to be a brain surgeon to help his brother.

    MUM'S HEARTACHE

    "We raised him to be Neel’s guardian in the future if anything was to happen to us. We taught him all the life skills to be a really good person.It was an immense shock."

    Neel, who has had brain damage since birth, recovered but stopped talking and walking when he discovered his younger brother had died.

    It comes after it was revealed 38 people have been killed on smart motorways in the past five years.

    The grim toll is blamed on the removal of hard shoulders, leaving drivers nowhere safe to stop when they break down.

    Near-misses on one stretch of the reconfigured M25 outside London rose 20-fold to 1,485 in the five years since the hard shoulder was taken away.

    I don’t want another family to see their child’s dead body in front of them

    Meera Naran

    The figures come from an investigation into the 200 miles of smart motorways by BBC’s Panorama, as the government urgently probes their safety.

    It also found that one warning sign on the the M25 had been out of action for 336 days.

    Smart motorways are sections of main roads that are monitored by technology to manage the flow of traffic in busy times.

    Operators can vary the speed limit - with lit signs on overhead gantries - with the aim of reducing the frustrating stop-start driving conditions that often occur on normal roads.

    Smart motorways can also increase capacity of the road by opening up the hard shoulder at busy times.

    DRIVERS IN DANGER

    Safety experts and MPs are calling for the controversial roads to be scrapped as drivers are being left stranded in dangerous situations on the side of the road.

    Recent figures have revealed more than 19,000 motorists have broken down in a live lane over the past two years, a rate of 26 drivers a day.

    The former minister who approved the idea said he was misled about the risks. Sir Mike Penning said: “They endanger people’s lives.”

    Transport Secretary Grant Shapps said: “We have to have these as safe or safer as regular motorways or we shouldn’t have them at all.”

    MOST READ IN NEWS

    Mrs Naran has called on the government to act over smart motorways.

    She added: "My ultimate aim for this is to save lives. I don’t want another family to lose their Dev.

    "I don’t want another family to see their child’s dead body in front of them."

    Smart motorway speed cams are being rolled out across the UK and can catch you even when no variable speed limit is displayed
