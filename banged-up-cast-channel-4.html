    Banged Up cast: Who stars in the Channel 4 show?

    BANGED Up follows a group of celebrities as they swap their normal lives for time behind bars at the decommissioned Shrewsbury Prison. Here is who you can expect to see on your screen for the Channel 4 series.

    12 Jun 2024, 00:00

    BANGED Up follows a group of celebrities as they swap their normal lives for time behind bars at the decommissioned Shrewsbury Prison. 

    Here is who you can expect to see on your screen for the Channel 4 series.

    Who is in the cast of Banged Up?

    Sid Owen

    EastEnders star Sid Owen is best known for playing Ricky Butcher in the BBC soap.

    He took part in season ten of Strictly Come Dancing and was paired with professional dancer Ola Jordan.

    They were the third pair to be eliminated from the competition.

    The actor claimed that he is the only person in his family not to have served time behind bars, but he will experience prison life first hand in the new Channel 4 series.

    Read More on Channel 4

    Tom Rosenthal

    Tom Rosenthal is a comedian known for playing Jonny in the Channel 4 sitcom Friday Night Dinner.

    He's also known for playing Marcus in the comedy series Plebs.

    In 2014 he co-created a comedy pilot called Flat TV, which was commissioned into a mini-series.

    He appeared in Guy Ritchie's film Operation Fortune: Ruse de Guerre, and voiced the lead role in the animated series Adventures Of ArachnoFly.

    Most read in TV

    Marcus Luther

    Marcus Luther is known for his larger-than-life personality.

    He is a Gogglebox star who has appeared on the show since 2018 alongside his wife Mica Ven.

    The pair announced that they were leaving the show last year.

    It came two months after they suffered a devastating death in the family.

     HRVY

    Pop singer HRVY has joined the line-up.

    He was a runner-up on season 18 of Strictly Come Dancing with partner Janette Manrara.

    Speaking of his experience filming, the singer told the Daily Star: "I go to a prison, and I live in a prison environment for like eight days, itâ€™s the maddest thing that I have ever done in my life."

    Peter Hitchens

    Mail on Sunday columnist Peter Hitchens is among seven celebrities to be banged up inside HMP Shrewsbury.

    He is known for his strong traditional conservative views and a belief in the rule of law.

    Following the filming process he wrote about how real he found the experience and how much he struggled during his time in the prison.

    Neil Parish

    Former Conservative MP Neil Parish is part of the all-male lineup.

    He resigned from his job last year after admitting to watching porn on his phone in the House of Commons.

    When he resigned he claimed that his behaviour had been immoral but not illegal.

    Johnny Mercer

    Sitting Conservative MP and Minister for Veterans' Affairs, Johnny Mercer will also be taking part in the show.

    He served in the British Army from 2002-2013, before becoming an MP in 2015.

    Mercer isn't a stranger to reality TV having won Celebrity Hunted in 2018.

    When is Banged Up on TV?

    Banged Up lands on Channel 4 on Tuesday, October 31, at 9.15pm.
