    Terrified British Airways passengers clap 'hero' pilot after emergency landing at Gatwick

    BRITISH Airways passengers said their pilot was a &quot;hero&quot; and gave him a round of applause after he was forced to make an emergency landing at Gatwick Airport. Flight BA2605, travelling from Naples, experienced technical difficulties over the English Channel.

    27 Aug 2024, 00:00

    BRITISH Airways passengers said their pilot was a "hero" and gave him a round of applause after he was forced to make an emergency landing at Gatwick Airport.

    Flight BA2605, travelling from Naples, experienced technical difficulties over the English Channel.

    The plane is understood to have suffered a technical glitch - reportedly a hydraulic fuel leak - in the air, and engineers were seen on the runway fixing the problem.

    TV presenter Hofit Golan was on board, and tweeted footage showing passengers applauding the pilot and his crew following the incident.

    She tweeted: "Wow. British Airways thank you for getting us home safely after making an emergency landing. Life is so fragile.

    "Blessed to be home safe."

    Fire crews met the plane when it landed.

    Incoming flights were temporarily diverted as the Airbus A320 plane was inspected.

    Four flights were sent to Birmingham, two to Stansted and two to Luton.

    The runway closed shortly after midnight and its standby runway was put into action - just as Frantic Friday got underway. The runway has since been reopened.

    Today is dubbed "Frantic Friday" as Brits across the country ready to kick off their summer holidays.

    As well as increased pressure at airports, motorists are also facing hefty delays with an estimated nine million leisure trips beginning this weekend.

    The RAC estimate that an extra 3.8million motorists will be on the roads.

    Another 3.2million cars will hit the road on Saturday, while congestion on Sunday will be slightly lighter with 2.2million.

    Flights are understood to be operating as normal in the meantime, however on social media Gatwick AIrport said: "We're expecting a very busy day so some delays might be possible."

    At airports passengers had taken to Twitter to report that their flights had been diverted to various airports across the country.

    One wrote: "Stranded in Luton EasyJet should have went to Gatwick, apparently flights have been diverted to Birmingham."

    To add to the drama, holidaymakers had been warned that they were facing a "summer of chaos" at Heathrow with passport queues getting so bad passengers are being forced to wait on the tarmac after landing.

    Airlines have demanded showdown talks with Ministers over a Border Force “staffing crisis”.

    Industry sources told The Sun that minimum time targets for passport queues had been breached an incredible 1,300 times so far this month.

    Passengers from non-EU countries such as America and South Africa are experiencing wait time of up to three-and-a-half  hours at the airport’s Terminal 4.

    And two weeks ago planes were forced to park by the terminal after landing at Britain’s biggest airport because it was too busy for passengers to disembark.

    On one day this week an insider claimed there were 17 fewer customs officials than required at Terminal 5.

    HIGHWAY TO HELL: Tips for avoiding traffic jams on the roads as Frantic Friday begins

    • Travel time: Most traffic queues are caused by too many cars on the same roads at the same time. If you can travel outside the peak times, you can easily miss them.
    • Be prepared: Make sure you conduct all necessary service checks on your motor before you set off. Many summer breakdowns are avoidable – punctures for instance can be caused by a tyre that is in poor condition or just not inflated properly. And ageing batteries can struggle in lots of stop-start traffic.
    • Keep it fresh: Hungry, thirsty or tired passengers are recipes for in-car irritability – and ‘carguments’. So when setting out, pack enough food and water to keep your passengers happy, and add in enough breaks along the way.
