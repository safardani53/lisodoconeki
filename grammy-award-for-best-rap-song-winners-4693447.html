    Hip-hop is arguably the most dominant music genre of the last half-century, eclipsing rock and roll to become the sound that makes the world go ’round. And some of the minds behind rap’s biggest songs get their flowers at the annual Grammy Awards, specifically in the Best Rap Song category. First held in 1958 and originally called the Gramophone Awards (aka those golden turntables that winners take home), the Grammy Awards have changed and evolved with the times, now celebrating “honor artistic achievement, technical proficiency, and overall excellence in the recording industry, without regard to album sales or chart position,” per the show’s website.

    With that said, the Grammys’ treatment of hip-hop hasn’t been…great. The Recording Academy debuted the Best Rap Performance award in 1989 and the Best Rap Album category in 1996, many years after the genre developed. The Grammys unveiled the Best Rap Song category for the 46th Grammy Awards in 2004. Eminem won that inaugural award for “Lose Yourself,” as did the song’s writers – Luis Restro and Jeff Bass.

    The Best Rap Song award is a songwriters award, one that recognizes the many hands that go into making a hit.

    2023’s Nominees

    The Best Rap Song category features some familiar names, with a few bright stars on the rise. DJ Khaled‘s “God Did,” featuring Rick Ross, Lil Wayne, Jay-Z, John Legend, and Fridayy, is nominated. Kendrick Lamar‘s “The Heart Part 5” is also up for the award, as is “Pushin P” by Gunna and Future featuring Young Thug.

    Future is one of the multiple nominees in this category. His “Wait For You,” featuring Tems and Drake, is also nominated. Drake pops back up again on “Churchill Downs,” the Jack Harlow song that also scored a nomination.

    In a non-broadcast, pre-show ceremony, Kendrick Lamar won. He also won Best Rap Performance, and Best Melodic Rap Song.
