    Jilted bride-to-be flogs wedding dress online after claiming fiance romped with her son's godmother.

    19 Sep 2024, 00:00

    A JILTED fiancee has tried to flog her wedding dress online after claiming he romped with her son's godmother.

    But Dave Notman, 26, furiously denies allegations he cheated with Nicole Martin, 23, after bride-to-be Kerry Wilkinson's cheeky ad went viral.

    Kerry, 27, previously offered the £500 gown for just £100 following her split with squaddie Dave.

    The mum, from Dunfermline, posted a snap of her wearing the frock with the message: "Sooner it's gone the better — just want every last bit of him out my life."

    Black Watch soldier Dave said: “There’s absolutely no truth in what she’s said. It’s quite embarrassing that it’s gone viral.

    “I was shaking with anger when I first saw the post, but I laughed it off after that.

    "I just want to clear my name because the whole of Scotland thinks I’m some sort of player. I’ve never cheated on anyone in my life.”

    He added: “I haven’t spoken to Kerry since we broke up before December so I don’t know what she’s playing at.

    “We were in an on-off relationship for two and a half years but we both agreed to call it a day. I haven’t spoken to her since.

    “Me and Nicole have never been together — she’s like a sister to me.”

    Nicole, of Ballingry, Fife, shot down the claims and branded her pal paranoid.

    She said: “I don’t even fancy David. I’ve been pals with him for years and nothing has ever happened between us.

    “The claims are ludicrous. It’s hilarious, the post is crazy.

    “She would make David stop speaking to us and delete us all off Facebook whenever they were together.

    "It’s all stemmed from paranoia.” She added: “Good luck to her, I hope she sells her wedding dress.”

    Kerry — mum to Logan, four — became an internet hit after posting on the Facebay Fife page.

    She wrote of the frock: “Never used due to ex fiancé receiving o*** off my sons god mother.”

    She added: “Bought from Bows & Bells in Kirkcaldy for £500 — just want every last bit of him out my life.”

    Last night Kerry refused to comment on the ad and affair allegations, which were posted on Thursday but have since been removed.

    But a close pal said she stood by the cheat claims.

    They said: “Kerry and Dave were together for about three years. They had their ups and downs but she didn’t deserve anything like this.

    “She told me it was like a dagger to the heart.

    “She wasn’t bothered about the big white wedding, but she loved her dress. It was perfect.

    “They were going to have a small registry wedding, nice holiday and then celebrate with their friends and family when they got home.”

    Kerry — who is planning to start university — met Dave in 2014 as she worked at a Dunfermline club.

    He was pals with her late brot­her Ross.

    Ker­ry and Dave got engaged in 2015.
