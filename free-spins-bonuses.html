Here are some of the most important factors to look out for: 

    • Bonus size - Before you rush to punch in those bonus codes, find out exactly what you’ll be getting. Free spin offers are either capped or uncapped.     • Payment methods - An online casino should offer users a varied selection of payment options. These must include all leading methods like VISA, Mastercard, Skrill, Neteller, and several others. Newer online casinos will even accept cryptocurrency. Another thing to note is that some free spin bonuses will exclude particular payment options.
    • Slot variety - The games you’re permitted to play with your bonus can mean the difference between success and failure. Older slot machines tend to pay out in very low amounts, and if your bonus can only be used on lower-quality games then it’s probably not worth your time. Look out for bonuses that come on flashy, big-name games to really ramp up your chances of success.
    • Safety measures - Your chosen online casino must be secure and set up to professionally protect your personal and financial information. Most online casinos will require you to make a deposit before you can trigger your free spins. But before revealing any sensitive information to the site, check the footer section for information about SSL encryption. 
    • Customer support - It’s to be expected that things go wrong from time to time, and when that does happen you need to be sure there’s a team on hand to help. Check out the online casino’s customer support service. Ideally you should have access to live chat that’s available 24 hours a day. 
    • Wagering requirements - Rollover requirements are a necessary evil of almost every bonus offer. The key is to make sure they’re fair and achievable. Ideally your wagering requirements are limited to 35x. Anything higher than this will make it really hard to turn bonus winnings into real, withdraw-able cash. 
    • Time limits - Your bonus will always come with an expiry date. You need to complete wagering within the time specified or the bonus will be cancelled and you’ll lose out on any wins generated with the bonus cash. Ideally, 14 to 30 days are required to give you enough time. Withdrawal restrictions might apply to some offers, so be sure to read the fine print.
