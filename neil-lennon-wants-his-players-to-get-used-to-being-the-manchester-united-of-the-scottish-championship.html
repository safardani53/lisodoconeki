    NEIL LENNON wants his players to get used to being the Manchester United of the Championship.

    Hibs boss Lenny knows he has walked into the biggest club currently outside of the top flight.

    His Scottish Cup winners are big favourites to go up this year and have sold 10,700 season tickets on the back of that Hampden triumph.

    Lennon reckons it’s time his men embraced that fact rather than let it overwhelm them, as has happened too often before.

    He was still smarting yesterday from Tuesday night’s shock Betfred Cup home defeat to Queen of the South.

    And ahead of tomorrow’s clash with Dunfermline, Lenny warned his side further lapses won’t be tolerated.

    He said: “This is a big club and they have to play like big players, develop that big club and game mentality.

    “I’m not too sure we’ve had that, but that’s what we are trying to get.

    “You go to Old Trafford in my playing days and you are up against Roy Keane, Ryan Giggs, David Beckham and Paul Scholes.

    “First of all you think ‘It must be easy playing in this team’ with Andy Cole and Dwight Yorke up front and Jaap Stam at the back.

    “Every team that went to Old Trafford raised their game because you want to play against the best.
    “These guys had to deal with that every week and it was the same at Celtic.

    “Everyone came to Celtic Park to raise their game — and then they would drop again the following week.
    “But when you are at a big club you have to maintain it.

    “We are now the big fish in this pond. So we have to make sure we’re ready for all teams coming and raising their game.”

    Lennon insists it will take time to get his Hibernian players thinking that way.

    But he is also confident he has enough with the right mentality to make it work.

    He said: “I’ve seen players go to the Old Firm and die. It takes a certain mentality to go and play for big clubs.

    “From my own experience I had Martin O’Neill and Gordon Strachan, great managers, and I had good players around me who maintained those levels.

    “I had good players when I was Celtic manager who could do it as well.

    “I do think we’ve got enough players capable of that.

    “Some of them already are in that mental state. What I want is for them to drag the others with them.
    “I want them to say: ‘Come on, let’s have a bit of this’. You have to deal with expectation.

    “We have had the chat. They are good playing against Premiership teams and they get themselves up for that.

    “I’m not saying they weren’t up for Queen of the South.

    “But there were lapses in concentration that maybe they wouldn’t make in other games.

    “We have to bring that intensity we would bring to big games to every domestic, day-to-day, bread and butter game.

    “If they do that, then they’ll be fine.”

    Hibs will still be without new keeper Ofir Marciano against the Pars as he waits for his paperwork to be completed.

    Lennon added: “George Craig is going over to Belgium next week to try to get it over the line for us.

    “Ofir had to sit an English test. He passed it — but I think Jason Cummings failed it!

    “But we’re nearly there. He’ll go away next week to get it all done and dusted.”
