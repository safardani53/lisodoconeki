    NEW YORK rapper A Boogie wit da Hoodie was forced to apologise to fans are he was arrested at Wireless Festival.

    The musician, who was also arrested in 2020, has accumulated millions of dollars since he burst onto the scene in 2016

    Who is A Boogie wit da Hoodie?

    A Boogie wit da Hoodie, real name Artist Dubose, is a rapper and singer.

    The 25-year-old, born in the Bronx, draws over 15million listeners a month on Spotify and has more than five million followers on Instagram.

    A Boogie topped the US album chart with his 2018 album Hoodie SZN, and his two other albums have made the Top 5.

    He has a daughter named Melody and a son named Artist Jr. with Instagram model Ella Rodriguez.

    Why was A Boogie Wit Da Hoodie arrested at Wireless festival?'

    The rapper was arrested on September 11 following an incident at the Wireless Festival in Crystal Palace Park, London.

    He apologised to his fans, explaining the incident on his social media after the organisers were forced to cancel his set.

    Writing online the rapper, 25, said: “Smh sorry to everyone who came out to see me at Wireless.

    "London police locked me up before I went on stage for something I was accused of 2 years ago. 

    “I’m OK and I’ll try my best to make it up to you guys."

    A Metropolitan Police spokesman added: “On Saturday, 11 September, a 25-year-old man was arrested at the Wireless Festival in Crystal Palace Park.

    “The arrest was made by Met Police officers at the request of Greater Manchester Police.

    “The man was released on bail to attend a Greater Manchester police station at a later date.”

    The incident came just nine months after the rapper was arrested following a raid on his house.

    The Sun reported that police had found drugs and a number of guns.

    He was charged with unlawful possession of a handgun and possession of marijuana after police executed a search warrant.
