    KYLIE Jenner showed off her "spoiled" three-year-old daughter Stormi's $3,455 shoe collection in an Instagram Story post on Tuesday.

    The impressive collection consists of Air Jordans and Nike Dunks.

    The shoes in the collection range from the $94 Dunk Low SP TD Ceramic sneakers to the higher end $475 Air Jordan 4 Retro GS Linen.

    Other shoes included in Stormi's impressive sneaker closet are the $220 Air Jordan 6 Retro GS Gatorade sneaker and $157 Starfish sneaker from the same line, as well as the Off-White x Rubber Dunk PS Green Strike, which cost $100, and the $120 Stussy x Air Force 1 Low PS Fossil shoe.

    Some of the sneakers are colorful- such as the Dunk High SP PS Iowa sneaker for $120- while others are more neutral, including the Air Jordan 4 Retro GS Black Cat that retails for $270.

    Stormi isn't the only one with an impressive closet, as mom Kylie also boasts a closet full of expensive bags and shoes.

    The Kylie Cosmetics founder's impressive closet features designer clutches worth $13k, a $4.5k Chanel bag and $1.2k Bottega Veneta pumps.

    The 23-year-old shared multiple Instagram posts showing off Chanel bags, most of which were vintage.

    She captioned one video: "Vintage Chanel."

    In the clip, the Keeping Up With the Kardashians star panned her camera across the shelves that were decked out with the stylish bags as Frank Ocean's 2017 song Chanel played in the background.

    Some of the bags date back to the 80s, while a number of other Chanel purses were from the 2000s.

    Kylie showed off a pink Round as Earth Bag, which has a retail price of $4.5k.

    Another bag featured in Kylie's collection was the Python Lait de Coco Minaudiere Box from 2014, which cost $5,196.

    An additional bag she put on display was a pearl-shaped round clutch bag from 2005, which had an original retail price of $3,750 but can now be bought for up to $10k.

    Kylie's collection wouldn't be complete without an extremely rare item, which happens to be the red Choco Bar Heart Shaped CC Clutch.

    Only 100 of the bags were ever made and were sold as a way to raise funds for victims of 9/11 in 2001.

    The extremely rare bag is now sold for up to $13k on resale websites.
